Web Exploitation Techniques

Web exploitation involves identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities in web applications. These weaknesses can lead to serious consequences if left unaddressed. Common vulnerabilities include Server-Side Request Forgery (SSRF), SQL Injection, Cross-Site Scripting (XSS), Local File Inclusion (LFI), and more. Capture-the-Flag (CTF) challenges often simulate real-world scenarios, allowing participants to practice their skills. Remember, understanding web vulnerabilities is crucial for anyone interested in cybersecurity! 🔐🌐

Steganography

Steganography is the art of hiding secret messages within seemingly ordinary digital content, such as images, audio files, or text, without attracting attention. Unlike encryption, which scrambles messages to make them unintelligible to unauthorized individuals, steganography focuses on concealing the existence of the message itself. The term "steganography" combines the Greek words "steganos" (meaning "covered" or "hidden") and "graphia" (meaning "writing" or "drawing"). 📜🕵️‍♂️💻
